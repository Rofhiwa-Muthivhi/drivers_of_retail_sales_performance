# Hypotheses

After defining the business problem and identifying the key business questions, the next step was to develop hypotheses.

A hypothesis is an **educated assumption** about a possible relationship in the data that will later be tested through analysis.

The purpose of hypotheses is to guide analysis by creating assumptions that can either be:

- Supported by data ✅  
- Rejected by data ❌  

Both outcomes are valuable because they improve business understanding.

In this project, hypotheses were developed based on the business questions and MECE framework.

---

## 1. Store Characteristics

### H1: Larger stores generate higher weekly sales.

It is expected that larger stores may generate stronger sales performance because they are likely to offer more products and serve more customers.

### H2: Store type influences sales performance.

It is expected that differences in store type (`A`, `B`, `C`) may influence weekly sales performance.

---

## 2. Economic Conditions

### H3: Higher unemployment negatively affects retail sales.

It is expected that higher unemployment may reduce customer spending power, which could negatively affect retail sales.

### H4: Higher CPI negatively affects customer spending.

It is expected that increases in CPI (inflation) may reduce customer purchasing power and affect sales performance.

### H5: Higher fuel prices reduce retail activity and sales.

It is expected that rising fuel prices may reduce customer travel and discretionary spending, which could influence retail sales.

---

## 3. Seasonal Effects

### H6: Holiday weeks generate higher sales than non-holiday weeks.

It is expected that holiday periods may increase customer spending and improve weekly sales performance.

### H7: Retail sales display seasonal patterns over time.

It is expected that some weeks or periods may consistently generate stronger sales than others.

---

## 4. Promotions

### H8: Promotional markdowns improve sales performance.

It is expected that promotional markdown campaigns may increase weekly sales.

### H9: Promotions are more effective during holiday periods.

It is expected that markdowns may generate stronger results when combined with holiday shopping periods.

---

## 5. Department Performance

### H10: Certain departments consistently outperform others.

It is expected that some departments may generate stronger and more consistent sales performance than others.

---

## Why Hypotheses Matter

Hypotheses help ensure that analysis is conducted with purpose.

Rather than exploring data randomly, SQL analysis will be used to test assumptions and determine whether expected relationships are supported by evidence.
